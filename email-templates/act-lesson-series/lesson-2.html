Math Logic

Logic questions on the math section of the ACT is the third most important topic. You will see 4 - 5 of these questions on every test. They constitute questions that require reasoning and logic or trial and error to solve rather than a direct analytical approach. Frequently, these questions will give definitions and / or constraints that must be observed.

To solve these, typically the best approach is trial and error. Thus, you will select numbers that fit the definitions or constraints and plug them into the answer choices to see which answer(s) remains. If more than 1 answer choice remains, select new numbers and plug them in again.

For example,

Given that p is a positive number, n is a negative number, and |p| < |n|, which of the following expressions has the greatest value?

A. |(p - n) / p|

B. |(p - n) / n|

C. |(p + n) / (p - n)|

D. |(p + n) / p|

E. |(p + n) / n|

In this problem, we have the constraints that n is negative, p is positive, and the magnitude (absolute value) of p is greater than n. Thus, let's try p = 3 and n = -2. If we plug these numbers into all 5 answer choices, we will get 5/3 for F, 5/2 for G, 1/5 for H, 1/3 for J, and 1/2 for K. If we compare these 5 answers to each other, we will see that 5/2 (G) is the largest, making G the correct answer.
