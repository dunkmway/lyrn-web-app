Math - Units

The units topic on the math section of the ACT is the second most important topic. You will see 5 - 6 of these questions on every test. They typically involve dimensional analysis, conversions, and rates. You can easily identify these questions as they will always involve units.

To solve these, you need to identify the starting and final units. You will then use the ratios and rates to convert from one unit to another. Be careful when converting units: make sure that all units can be found in the numerator and denominator of each fraction except for your final units. There are several conversions that the ACT won't give to you because you are expected to know them: 60 seconds in a minute, 60 minutes in an hour, 24 hours in a day, 12 inches in a foot, 3 feet in a yard, etc.

For example,

How many minutes would it take a train to travel 90 kilometers at a constant speed of 120 kilometers per hour?

A. 30


B. 40


C. 45


D. 80


E. 90

We can identify this question as a unit question because of the various units found in the problem: minutes, kilometers per hour, and kilometers. Thus, we'll start by identifying our starting units (90 km) and our final units (minutes-solving for this). In our set of conversions, we need to add the conversion of 60 minutes per hour. Now that we have our starting and final units and our conversions, we'll write our conversions:

90 km
1 hr
120 km
60 min
1 hr
=
45 min

Thus, once we multiply everything together, we get 45 minutes: answer choice C.
