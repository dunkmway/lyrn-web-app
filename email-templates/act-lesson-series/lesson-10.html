Math - Polygons

The polygons topic on the math section of the ACT is the most important topic. You will see 6 - 8 of these questions on every test. These questions will ask you to find the area, perimeter, circumference, surface area, or volume of a given shape or object. Some of the important equations to know are the area of a triangle, area of a rectangle, area of a circle, perimeter of a rectangle, and circumference of a circle.

These questions vary greatly but solving typically involves writing down applicable equations, given values, plugging in known values, and / or linking one equation to another.

For example,

One side of square ABCD has a length of 12 meters. A certain rectangle whose area is equal to the area of ABCD has a width of 8 meters. What is the length, in meters, of the rectangle?

A. 12


B. 16


C. 18


D. 20


E. 24

We know that this is a polygon question because it asks us to find the area of a square and that of a rectangle. We'll start by writing applicable equations down: Area of a square = b ⋅ b; Area of a rectangle = l ⋅ w; Area of square = Area of rectangle. Next, we'll plug in values that we have: b = 12 --> Area of square = 12 ⋅ 12 = 144. Thus, the Area of both the square and rectangle is 144. Using the equation of a rectangle we get 144 = l ⋅ 8; dividing both sides by 8, we get l = 18: answer C.
