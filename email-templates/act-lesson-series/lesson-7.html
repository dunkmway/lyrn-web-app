Reading - Context Clues

In the reading section of the ACT, they frequently use uncommon words or alternate meanings to common words. Thus, it is important to be able to infer the meaning of words based on context. When you come across a word whose meaning you do not know (whether reading a book, news article, or other literature), you should take a second to try and identify its meaning. Using the surrounding words and sentence, try to think of another word, if replaced with the word in question, that would make sense. By doing this you create a word association between the two words that will improve your vocabulary. Additionally, by incorporating this technique into your every day reading, you could greatly enhance your performance on the reading section of the ACT.

For example,

Passage Text: To clinch the issue, she proposes that archaeologists undertake an extensive search for the burnt remains of P. esculenta and other pit-roasted plants, since charring would have protected them from chemical or bacterial degradation.

The word clinch (underlined) most nearly means to:

A. settle or resolve.

B. stick to.

C. grasp or recognize.

D. fasten firmly on.

In this case, we are talking about an issue for which someone (likely a scientist) is proposing a solution to. In the context of providing a solution to an issue, the word I might use to replace 'clinch' with would be 'address' or 'fix'. The answer choice that is most similar to my selected word(s) is A: settle or resolve.
