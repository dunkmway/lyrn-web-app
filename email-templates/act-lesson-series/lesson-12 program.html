Science - Graph Reading

Graph Reading is the most important topic on the Science section of the ACT. You can expect to see 8 - 12 of these questions per test. These questions are usually straightforward, they provide a value in the question and ask you to find another (typically in the same graph or table, but not always).

To solve these, simply identify the graph or table that contains the value provided in the question and the graph or table that contains the variable you are trying to solve for. If they are found within the same graph or table, then you will only use the information in that graph or table. If they are in separate graphs or tables, then there will always be a variable that links the two together.

For example,

According to Table 2, by approximately how many degrees does the boiling point of a linear hydrocarbon differ from that of a branched hydrocarbon with the same number of carbon atoms?

A. 5°C

B. 10°C

C. 20°C

D. 30°C

Here, both the boiling point of a linear hydrocarbon and the boiling point of a branched hydrocarbon can be found in the same table. If we look at each hydrocarbon length and we compare the two boiling points, we will get a difference of 12 for 4 carbon atoms, 8 for 5 carbon atoms, 9 for 6 carbon atoms, and 8 for 7 carbon atoms. Looking at our answer choices, a difference of 8, 9, or 12 are all closest to a difference of 10°C: answer choice B.
