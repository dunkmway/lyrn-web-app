Science - Reasoned Answer

Reasoned Answer is the third most important topic on the Science section of the ACT. As such, you can expect to see 4 to 6 of these questions on every test. You can identify these questions by looking at the answers first. They will contain 2 different answers (yes, yes, no, no; Option A, Option A, Option B, Option B; etc.) with reasons.

To solve these, start with the reasons for each answer choice. If the reason is true, then leave it as a possibility. If it's false, then cross out the answer. Do this for all four answer choices. If there is only one answer choice left, you are finished. If two answer choices remain, then read the question, and, using the information gained from the answer choices, select the correct option.

For example,

Experiment 4

Four test tubes containing equal amounts of S. epidermidis and 1.0 mL of sterile water were subjected to different temperatures. Samples from each tube were taken periodically. Then the samples were washed, incubated, and checked for bacterial growth as described above. The results appear in Table 4.

Table 4
Time (min) Temperature (°C)
20 40 60 100
2.5 + + + +
5.0 + + + -
10.0 + + + -
15.0 + + + -

Is the conclusion that "E. coli does not grow well after heating at 100°C supported by the results of Experiment 4?"

A. No; after 2.5 min of exposure, bacterial growth occurred.

B. No; E. coli was not used in Experiment 4.

C. Yes; after 5.0 min of exposure, bacterial growth was stopped.

D. Yes; E. coli growth is stopped by NG.

We know that this is a reasoned answer question because there are only two answers (Yes or No) with a reason for each one. Thus, we will start by looking at each reason. Answer choices A and C are both wrong because Experiment 4 doesn't look at time of exposure (this is actually part of experiment 5). Answer choice D is wrong for a similar reason: NG is part of another experiment. Thus, the only remaining answer is B. We can verify that this is the correct answer by skimming the text in Experiment 4 (before the table) to see that this experiment uses S. epidermidis.
