English - Connotation

Connotation is the third most important topic on the English section of the ACT. As such, you can expect to see 5 - 7 of these questions on every test. They will ask you to select the answer with the correct connotation as specified in the question. Moreover, the best way to recognize a connotation question is to pay attention to words and phrases like 'emphasizes', 'clearest', 'most specific', and 'best indicates'.

To solve these, find the key word or phrase in the question and read to the end of the sentence. Afterwards, select the answer choice that addresses the goal using the correct connotation.

For example,

At this point, the writer wants to emphasize the idea that the narrator found the woman's comment peculiar. Which of the following best accomplishes that goal?

A. I figured she had been to the caves before.

B. Surprised, I hesitantly turned toward her.

C. She had a notepad in her hand.

D. I happened to agree.

We know that this is a connotation question because of the word 'emphasize'. If we read to the end of the sentence, we learn that our goal is to 'emphasize the idea that the narrator found the woman's comment peculiar.' Another word for peculiar is strange or odd. Thus, the only answer choice that indicates that the woman's comment was strange is B.
