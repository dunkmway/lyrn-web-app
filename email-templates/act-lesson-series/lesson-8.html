Science - Conflicting Viewpoint

Conflicting Viewpoints is the second most important topic on the Science section of the ACT. Rather than being a question type, conflicting viewpoints is a passage type that will always contain 7 questions. This passage rarely contains tables, charts, or figures but rather contains various 'viewpoints': usually students, scientists, or hypotheses.

When approaching this section, you will begin by skimming each question and labeling them according to the viewpoint(s) required to answer the question: 1, 2, 1 + 3, All, None, etc. Once all questions have been labeled, read the intro text then answer any questions labeled with an N (None). Next, read viewpoint one then answer the questions labeled with 1; read viewpoint 2 and answer questions labeled with 2 then questions labeled with 1 and 2. Continue this process until all questions have been answered.

For example,

Student 2 indicates that the hyphae of a GPM are primarily involved in producing:

A. energy.

B. spores.

C. toxins.

D. offspring.

In this question, we see that we are concerned with Student 2's viewpoint, and only their viewpoint. Thus, we would label this question with a 2 and would answer this question after we read student 2's point of view.
